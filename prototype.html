Frequently Asked Questions

What can CyberScan check?

CyberScan checks common web application risks such as missing security headers, exposed sensitive files, insecure cookies, visible dependency versions, form risks, and TLS issues.

It is designed for authorized testing against systems you own or have permission to assess.

Which scan type should I choose?

Use Quick for a short baseline check, Full for broader unauthenticated coverage, and Authenticated when you need session-aware testing behind login.

Open scan setup

How does crawl depth affect a scan?

Higher depth allows the scanner to follow more discovered links, which increases coverage and runtime. Depth 3 is the balanced default for most small to medium web apps.

Where are generated reports saved?

The local web app saves generated JSON, CSV, and HTML reports in the project reports folder. Recent reports also appear on the Reports page.

View reports

Can reports be scheduled automatically?

Yes. Use the Schedule button on a report type. Weekly schedules run while app.py is running, and schedule settings are stored in reports/automation.json.

What should I do with a high severity result?

Confirm the affected URL and evidence, mark it for owner review, fix the root cause, and rerun the scan. The Reports page includes prevention guidance generated from the latest results.

Why might a scan fail?

Common causes include an unreachable target, blocked outbound network access, malformed URLs, certificate errors, or a scan that was cancelled before completion.
